Mahendra Singh Dhoni

From small-town boy to global icon, MS Dhoni's journey is a testament to perseverance and cool-headed leadership

"Here's a time-line of MS Dhoni's life"

  • 2004:-Dhoni makes his international debut against Bangladesh in an ODI at Chittagong
  • 2005:-Dhoni smashes an unbeaten 183 off 145 balls,the then record of the highest individual score in ODI cricket in the second innings.
  • 2007:-Dhoni takes over the reins of ODI captaincy from Rahul Dravid.
  • 2008:-Dhoni leads India to its first-ever bilateral ODI series victory in Sri Lanka.
  • 2008:-Dhoni wins the ICC ODI Player of the Year honour
  • 2009:-Under Dhoni's captaincy, India tops the ICC Test cricket rankings for the first time
  • 2010:-Dhoni leads Chennai Super Kings to their maiden Indian Premier League title.
  • 2011:-Dhoni plays his most memorable match-winning unbeaten knock -- 91 off 79 -- balls against Sri Lanka in the World Cup final to help India lift the trophy after 28 years.
  • 2013:-Led India to ICC Champions Trophy win
  • 2013:-Dhoni becomes the most successful Indian Test captain, eclipses Sourav Ganguly's record of 21 victories from 49 Tests
  • 2018:-Dhoni receives the Padma Bhushan, India's third-highest civillian honour
  • 2020:-Dhoni retired from International Cricket on 15th August
  • 2023:-Led CSK to fifth IPL Title win under his captaincy
